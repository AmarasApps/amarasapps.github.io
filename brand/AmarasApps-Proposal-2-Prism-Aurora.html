01

Why Prism × Aurora works

CONCEPT

This is the most conceptually unified pairing in the folio — and deliberately so. A prism takes one input and refracts it into a spectrum; Aurora is a spectrum (violet → magenta → coral). The mark and the palette tell the identical story: one studio, many colourful outputs. That gives the brand a reason to exist beyond decoration — and hands you a ready-made system for sub-brands.

What it signals

Creative, versatile, modern, category-agnostic. This reads “studio that could make a game, a tool, or an app” — the opposite of a single-purpose finance brand. If your roadmap might include playful or creative products, this is the strongest fit.

The discipline that makes it safe

Bold palettes fail when everything shouts. The rule here: the studio owns the full spectrum; each product owns exactly one colour split from it. Body text always sits on near-white / near-black neutrals. The spectrum is for the mark, gradients, glows and accents — never long-form text.

The one hard accessibility rule (measured, not guessed): the bright spectrum colours are luminous — they pass AAA as text/glow on dark and as fills behind dark text, but they fail as white-text button fills and as coloured text on white. So: bright accent = dark-ink button or dark-bg glow; for a coloured button with white text on light pages, use the deep violet #7C3AED (AA 5.7). Every mockup in this proposal obeys this.

Proposal 2 — at a glance

SUMMARY

A single, committed direction: Prism mark · Aurora palette · spectrum-refraction sub-brand system. The most conceptually unified and expressive option — best if AmarasApps may span many categories (including playful/creative products), and it hands you a scalable per-product colour system out of the box.

Strengths

• One idea across mark + palette + sub-brands.
• Category-agnostic, creative, memorable.
• Built-in per-product accent system.
• Fully WCAG-verified.

Trade-offs

• Boldest palette — needs the discipline rules.
• Bright hues are fill/glow only (never white-text buttons).
• Less “serious/fintech” than a blue direction.

Best if…

Your roadmap may include games, creative or consumer apps — not only finance/utility. If you want maximum gravitas instead, Proposal 1's Monolith/Nebula is safer.

If you choose this — remaining picks
Finish

Gradient (default) · or Liquid-glass for the hero/icon. Flat violet always ships as the mono secondary.

Product hues

Confirm Sparepay = gold. Reserve emerald / sky / amber / coral for future apps (all AAA-verified).
