AMARASAPPS · CONFIDENTIAL DRAFT
01

Studio ≠ Product

STRATEGY

The key correction in this revision: AmarasApps and Sparepay are two different brands. AmarasApps is the studio — it should feel like a versatile maker of software of any category (a game, a utility, a creative tool — not only finance, not only “private on-device”). Sparepay is one product with its own finance-flavoured identity (navy + gold). They are related the way a publisher relates to a title — connected by quality and a small “by AmarasApps” endorsement, not by sharing a colour scheme.

AmarasApps — the studio

Personality: modern, versatile, technical, optimistic. Category-neutral.
Palette family: cool indigo / violet / electric-blue “software” tones (proposed on the next pages) — deliberately not Sparepay's navy+gold.
Where it appears: the amarasapps.com landing page, the footer of every product, “by AmarasApps” endorsements, social/OG cards.

Sparepay — a product

Personality: calm, trustworthy, money-smart.
Palette: its own — navy #133244 + gold #F5C542 (from its icon).
Relationship: lives on its own product page under the studio site, carries a small AmarasApps endorsement mark, but keeps its distinct look. Future products do the same, each with their own accent.

The connective tissue is the system, not the colour: shared typography (SF Pro Rounded), shared layout language, shared motion, and the AmarasApps logo as a consistent endorsement. This lets a bright game and a serious finance app both sit under the studio without clashing.

Logo concepts

MARKS

Four distinct directions for the AmarasApps mark. All are built as scalable SVG, all resolve to a clean silhouette at favicon size, and all read as an “A” or a studio idea without naming a category. Shown here on their native dark tiles.

PEAK · bold gradient A
ORBIT · system / platform
PRISM · faceted A
STACK · layered glass
Peak

A confident monogram “A”. Reads instantly, works at any size, most flexible. Safest, most timeless.

Meaning: ascent, the letter A.

Orbit

A core + orbiting satellite. Says “platform / system of things.” Category-neutral & distinctive.

Meaning: a studio things orbit around.

Prism

A faceted “A” refracting light — one input, many outputs (many products). Techy, premium.

Meaning: creativity, refraction.

Stack

Layered glass sheets = a stack of apps. Most literal “studio” story; liquid-glass native.

Meaning: a collection / a suite.

04

Rendering styles

FINISH

Form is one axis; finish is another. The same mark can be delivered flat (versatile, print-safe), gradient (default, modern), liquid-glass (depth + highlight, matches Apple's current language), or skeuomorphic (rich, tactile). Here the “Peak” A is shown in all four so you can judge finish independently of concept.

FLAT · single colour, print-safe
GRADIENT · default, modern
LIQUID GLASS · translucent + sheen
SKEUOMORPHIC · glossy, tactile
Recommendation on finish

Gradient as the primary (App Store icon, site). Flat as the mandatory secondary — for monochrome, print, embroidery, tiny sizes, and the “by AmarasApps” endorsement. Liquid-glass for hero/marketing moments and if you want to echo Apple's current OS aesthetic. Skeuomorphic is beautiful but dates faster and is harder to keep crisp — offer it only as an alternate app-icon.

Monochrome / endorsement test
Knockout works both ways — essential for a studio mark that appears on every product.

Decisions to make

NEXT

To move from folio to a built site, I need three picks. All combinations below are pre-validated for contrast, so choose on feel.

1 · Logo concept

Peak · Orbit · Prism · Stack.
My pick: Orbit (best studio story, unique) or Peak (safest, most flexible).

2 · Finish

Flat · Gradient · Liquid-glass · Skeuomorphic.
My pick: Gradient primary + Flat secondary for endorsement/mono.

3 · Studio palette

Nebula · Monolith · Aurora.
My pick: Nebula — distinct from Sparepay, premium, versatile.

Once picked, I'll: (1) finalise the chosen SVG in the chosen finish + a flat/mono variant, (2) write the studio tokens to BRAND.md + a CSS :root, then (3) build the multi-product amarasapps.com — a polished landing page with the studio brand, per-product sub-pages (Sparepay first, keeping its navy+gold), scroll animation and depth, all WCAG-compliant.
My overall recommendation

Orbit mark, gradient finish, Nebula palette — a category-neutral studio identity that is unmistakably not a finance brand, leaves room for games/tools/anything next, and lets Sparepay keep its own navy+gold without conflict.
