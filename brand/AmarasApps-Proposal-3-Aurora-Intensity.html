“Modern & trustworthy” — with Aurora

THE BRIEF

Aurora is the expressive palette, and “trustworthy” is the one quality bright spectrums don't shout by default. The good news: colourful brands can be deeply trusted (Stripe, Figma, Raycast) — they earn it through disciplined application, not by going grey. The lever is how much of the spectrum you actually show, and which hue leads.

What makes it trustworthy

• Colour is seasoning, not the meal — text always on neutrals.
• A grounded lead hue (deep violet) rather than hot pink.
• Deep aubergine canvas → “aurora at night”, sophisticated not candy.
• Mature layout, generous space, restrained motion.

What makes it modern

• Spectrum gradient in the mark + one hero moment.
• Current, premium feel (violet is the “tech-premium” hue of the moment).
• Distinct from every blue B2B SaaS brand.
• A built-in, colourful sub-brand system.

Both options below are fully WCAG-verified. The only difference is temperature: how much magenta/coral you let in, and whether the primary button is grounded violet or vivid. Judge on feel.

My recommendation

PICK

You said modern & trustworthy, and you chose Aurora. Those two facts point clearly at one answer.

A · Violet-anchored Aurora

Keep Aurora's identity — the spectrum lives in the mark, the sub-brand hues, and one hero gradient — but run the UI on grounded deep violet. This is the “aurora at night” read: modern and distinctive because it's violet not blue, trustworthy because it's disciplined and grounded, not candy-bright. It also makes the per-product accent colours (Sparepay gold, etc.) pop harder, because the studio chrome stays calm.

Choose A (violet-anchored) if…

You want people to trust the studio with serious products (finance, data), you like Aurora's look but want it to feel premium/mature, and you want products' own colours to stand out.

Choose B (full spectrum) if…

You want maximum expressiveness and memorability, expect playful/consumer/creative products, and “distinctive” matters more to you than “sober”.

My pick for your brief: A — Violet-anchored Aurora. It's the only version that satisfies both your words. We keep the beautiful spectrum where it earns attention (logo, sub-brands, hero) and stay grounded everywhere trust is built (buttons, text, layout). Full spectrum is always available later for a campaign or a playful product.

If you approve A, the next decisions get fast: logo finish (gradient vs liquid-glass Prism), confirm product hues (Sparepay = gold), and wordmark (SF Pro Rounded vs custom). Then we build the site.
